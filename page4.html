What can we do?

Change....

We need to start helping our oceans. We can do this by recycling, cutting emissions, moving to green energy and ensuring waste does not enter the oceans. Reduce our plastic use, plastic is a very useful human invention it can be used for containers, medical equipment, car parts and so much more. Yet when it is dumped it washes into the rivers due to its buoyancy and floats out to sea where it is eaten by fish and other animals causing severe issues this is of course accidental on the fishes behalf but to us its not we have a choice. We can reuse the plastic or for better stop producing it as to produce it causes massive greenhouse pollution which causes acid rain to fall into our oceans. We can use other less harmful materials such as bamboo, wood, natural fibre and also bioplastics these are all sustainable.


Start cleaning our beaches, removing harmful debris and materials that have washed up disposing of them in the correct manner. This would be a great thing to do for school children on summer holidays as part of an awareness campaign. We need to get serious about recycling. In Ireland we have Return where empty cans and bottles are bought with a 15 or a 25 cent charge on top of the cost to incentivise people to return them to their local machine for the return of their charge. This is a great initiative and it works! We need to hold countries responsible for their actions. This is extremely important. The worlds biggest economies are the biggest polluters. This needs to change. As you can see from this graph of the biggest polluters according to Worldometer: https://www.worldometers.info/co2-emissions/co2-emissions-by-country/.
